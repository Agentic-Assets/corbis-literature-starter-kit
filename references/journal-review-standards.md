# Journal-Specific Review Standards

Agent 6 should adapt its evaluation to the target journal's norms. Key differences:

## Finance journals

**JF**: Highest bar for contribution — must change how the profession thinks about a topic. Concise writing, tight identification, broad relevance. Desk rejection rate ~75%.

**JFE**: Slightly more room for careful empirical work on important questions. Values clean identification and thorough robustness. Strong data papers welcome.

**RFS**: Comparable to JF/JFE. Dual-submission option with conferences. Values novelty and methodological rigor.

**JFQA**: Accepts careful empirical work on narrower questions. Good outlet for papers with strong execution but more focused contribution.

**Management Science**: Cross-disciplinary audience. Values clear economic intuition and broad relevance. More open to behavioral and experimental work.

**RoF (Review of Finance)**: Innovative, cross-disciplinary, or emerging finance topics. Receptive to careful empirical work on questions that bridge subfields. European Finance Association flagship.

## Real estate / urban journals

**REE (Real Estate Economics)**: Values institutional knowledge and policy relevance. Abstracts should be readable by non-specialists. No numbered sections (typically).

**JREFE (Journal of Real Estate Finance and Economics)**: Longer abstracts (150-250 words) with keywords. More technical detail acceptable. Footnotes used more freely.

**JRER (Journal of Real Estate Research)**: Business-facing real estate audience. Values decision-making relevance and practical implications for real estate professionals.

**JHE (Journal of Housing Economics)**: Housing-specific. Values policy relevance and careful measurement of housing market outcomes.

**RSUE (Regional Science and Urban Economics)**: Urban, spatial, and regional economics. Values clear spatial dimensions, general equilibrium thinking, and location-based identification.

**JUE (Journal of Urban Economics)**: Broader urban economics framing. Values spatial identification and general equilibrium thinking.

## Economics journals

**AER**: Broadest economics audience. Must be accessible to non-specialists. Strong theory-empirics connection.

**QJE**: Highest emphasis on big, surprising findings. Clean identification. Often shorter, more focused papers.

**JPE**: Values deep economic reasoning. More tolerant of structural approaches. Strong theory papers welcome.

**Econometrica**: Methodological rigor paramount. Theory or structural estimation papers. Formal proofs expected.

**REStud**: Comparable to top-5. Values innovation in either theory or methods. Longer papers acceptable.

## Common desk-rejection triggers (all journals)

- Contribution unclear or incremental
- Identification strategy unconvincing
- Paper too long for what it delivers
- Literature positioning inadequate (closest paper not acknowledged)
- Writing quality below journal standard
- Tables incomplete or inconsistent with text
