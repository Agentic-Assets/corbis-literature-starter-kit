# Seminar Deck Outline

## Slide 1: Title and contribution
- **Title**: [paper title]
- **Authors**: [names and affiliations]
- **One-sentence contribution**: [what the audience will learn]
- **Speaker note**: State the contribution clearly in the first 30 seconds. Do not save it for the end.
- **Time target**: 1 min

## Slide 2: Motivation
- **Key message**: [Why should the audience care? Real-world context.]
- **Visual**: [Motivating statistic, news headline, trend chart, or institutional example]
- **Speaker note**: Connect to something the audience already knows or cares about.
- **Time target**: 2 min

## Slide 3: Research question
- **Question**: [Precise, testable statement]
- **Speaker note**: Make sure the audience can hold this question in their head for the rest of the talk.
- **Time target**: 1 min

## Slide 4: Institutional setting / mechanism
- **Key message**: [The economic force that generates the prediction]
- **Visual**: [Timeline, institutional diagram, or mechanism schematic]
- **Speaker note**: This is where you build the audience's intuition. Do not rush.
- **Time target**: 3-5 min

## Slide 5: Preview of results (optional — required for conferences)
- **Key message**: [One-sentence summary of the main finding with magnitude]
- **Speaker note**: Conferences need this early. Seminars can defer.
- **Time target**: 1 min

## Slide 6: Identification challenge
- **Key message**: [What makes this question hard? What would a naive approach get wrong?]
- **Speaker note**: Acknowledge the challenge before presenting the solution. This builds credibility.
- **Time target**: 2 min

## Slide 7: Research design
- **Key message**: [How the design solves the identification challenge]
- **Visual**: [Design diagram, timeline of treatment/control, or map of variation]
- **Specification**: [Simplified equation — only the key terms]
- **Speaker note**: This is the slide where skeptics decide whether to trust you.
- **Time target**: 3-5 min

## Slide 8: Data and sample
- **Key message**: [What data, what sample period, how many observations]
- **Visual**: [Sample flow or summary statistics (simplified)]
- **Speaker note**: Keep brief. One slide unless the data are a contribution.
- **Time target**: 2 min

## Slide 9: Main result
- **Key message**: [The main finding]
- **Visual**: [Coefficient plot, event-study graph, or simplified table (1-3 columns max)]
- **Speaker note**: Show the result, then explain. Do not read the table aloud.
- **Time target**: 3-5 min

## Slide 10: Economic magnitude
- **Key message**: [Translate the coefficient into real-world units]
- **Speaker note**: "A one-SD increase in X leads to a Y% change, which is equivalent to..."
- **Time target**: 2 min

## Slide 11: Mechanism evidence
- **Key message**: [Why the effect operates through channel A, not B]
- **Visual**: [Heterogeneity pattern, intermediate outcome, or channel test]
- **Speaker note**: Be honest about what the mechanism evidence can and cannot show.
- **Time target**: 3 min

## Slide 12: Key robustness
- **Key message**: [2-3 checks that address the most important threats]
- **Visual**: [Summary table or coefficient stability plot]
- **Speaker note**: Name the threat, show the check, move on. Do not dwell.
- **Time target**: 2 min

## Slide 13: Conclusion
- **Key message**: [Restate the contribution, the economic lesson, and one forward-looking implication]
- **Speaker note**: Do not end with "thank you." End with the idea.
- **Time target**: 2 min
