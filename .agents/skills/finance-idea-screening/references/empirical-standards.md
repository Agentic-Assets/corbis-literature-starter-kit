# Empirical Standards for Idea Screening

Use this checklist to quickly assess whether an idea can meet publication-grade empirical standards.

## Design feasibility check
- Can you define the estimand in one sentence?
- Is there a plausible source of exogenous or quasi-exogenous variation?
- Can you name the comparison group and explain why it is informative?
- Can you write a baseline specification? If not, the idea is too vague.

## Data feasibility check
- Does the data exist and can you access it within a reasonable timeframe?
- Is the sample large enough for the proposed design? (Rule of thumb: N > 1000 for cross-sectional, T > 20 for time-series, cluster count > 50 for clustered designs.)
- Are the key variables measurable, or do they require heroic proxies?
- Are identifiers available for the merges you need?
- Is there look-ahead bias risk in the proposed variable construction?

## Identification reality check
- Does the proposed design actually require the proposed method, or would a simpler approach suffice?
- Is the identification assumption economically motivated or just statistically convenient?
- Would a skeptical referee buy the identification story in the first 5 minutes of a seminar?
- Are there obvious threats that cannot be addressed?

## Contribution reality check
- Can you state the contribution in one sentence without using "first" or "novel"?
- Does the result change how the field thinks about something, or is it merely confirming?
- Would the result surprise a well-informed researcher in the field?
- Is the question broad enough for the target journal but narrow enough to be answerable?

## Common idea killers
- The variation is endogenous and no credible instrument or natural experiment is available.
- The data are too noisy or too small for the magnitude of the expected effect.
- The closest paper already answers the question convincingly with better data or identification.
- The mechanism cannot be distinguished from obvious alternatives.
- The result would be too local or too descriptive for a top journal.
