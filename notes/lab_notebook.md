### 2026-04-04 — Literature Review: Political Connections in Finance

**What was done**: Comprehensive literature review on the importance of political connections in finance. Searched ~55 papers via Corbis across 7 search queries, reviewed abstracts and citation counts, organized into 5 thematic strands with citation-based tiering (10 Foundational / 9 Established / 6 Emerging).

**Strands covered**:
- Measuring the value of political connections (Fisman 2001 through RDD and surprise-election designs)
- Preferential access to finance and capital allocation (Khwaja-Mian through Houston et al.)
- Bailouts, government contracts, and regulatory favoritism (Faccio et al. 2006 through Bertrand et al. 2020)
- The dark side: innovation, creative destruction, and aggregate costs (Akcigit et al. 2023, Faccio-McConnell 2025)
- The revolving door and information channels (Emery-Faccio 2025, Jagolinzer et al. 2020)

**Key gaps identified**:
- Depreciation of political connections over time as regimes and personnel change
- Limited evidence on household-level financial outcomes (mortgages, consumer credit)
- Underexplored role of digital transparency and social media as checks on political influence

**Key conflicts identified**:
- Revolving door: "knowledge" vs. "capture" interpretations (Shive-Forster 2017 vs. Emery-Faccio 2025)

**Output files**:
- Review: paper/academic_paper_template.tex (12-page LaTeX document)
- Bibliography: paper/political_connections.bib (31 entries)
- Reading list: notes/reading_list_political_connections.md
- Compiled PDF: paper/academic_paper_template.pdf
