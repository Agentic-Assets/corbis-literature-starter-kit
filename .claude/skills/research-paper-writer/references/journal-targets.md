# Journal Target Notes

Use these notes as a practical targeting aid, not as a substitute for checking the latest submission page.

## Finance track

### The Journal of Finance (JF)
- Initial submission is single-blind.
- Title page with author names and abstract of no more than 100 words.
- Conflict-of-interest disclosure page follows the title page.
- Manuscript must not exceed 60 pages, including references, tables, figures, and internal appendices.
- Formatting: at least 1.5 line spacing, 12-point font, 1-inch side margins, 1.5-inch top and bottom margins.
- Internet Appendix may be attached at the end; does not count toward page limit.
- Paper should be self-contained without the appendix.
- Typical paper: broad-interest question, tight design, concise exposition, clear economic mechanism.

### Journal of Financial Economics (JFE)
- Requires separate title page PDF and anonymous manuscript PDF.
- Anonymous manuscript includes title and abstract of no more than 100 words.
- Double-spaced, 12-point or larger font, at least 1-inch margins.
- Online appendices attached to end of main manuscript at initial submission.
- Editors warn that weak grammar and syntax can lead to desk rejection.
- Typical paper: sharp empirical design or tight theoretical contribution, emphasizes economic magnitude.

### Review of Financial Studies (RFS)
- Title page plus manuscript files.
- Abstract no more than 100 words.
- Chicago author-date citation style.
- Footnotes kept to a minimum.
- Equations, tables, and figures clearly numbered and self-explanatory.
- Typical paper: similar to JF/JFE but sometimes slightly more receptive to methodological innovation or unconventional topics.

### Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis (JFQA)
- Abstract approximately 100-150 words.
- Double-spaced, standard formatting.
- Slightly more receptive to careful empirical work on focused questions than the top-3.
- Good outlet for solid identification with a narrower audience.

### Management Science (MS)
- Abstract up to 250 words.
- Accepts finance papers, especially those with broader management or behavioral implications.
- More flexible on length and structure than JF/JFE/RFS.
- Values interdisciplinary appeal and methodological rigor.

### Review of Corporate Finance Studies (RCFS)
- Focused on corporate finance topics.
- Good outlet for papers that are strong but narrower in scope than top-3 journals require.

### Journal of Banking and Finance (JBF)
- Broader acceptance criteria; good for solid empirical work without top-3 level novelty.
- Longer abstracts permitted.

## Real estate track

### Real Estate Economics (REE)
- Abstract: 100 words, italicized, readable by a layperson, independent of the paper.
- Block format, double spacing.
- Do not number sections.
- Keep the introduction brief.
- Tables and figures self-explanatory, on separate pages.
- Regression tables: coefficients and t-statistics in separate columns preferred.
- Report t-statistics rather than standard errors when possible.
- Flagship journal of AREUEA. Values institutional knowledge, clean identification, and policy relevance.

### Journal of Real Estate Finance and Economics (JREFE)
- Abstract: 150-250 words with 4-6 keywords.
- Author-year citations; alphabetized reference list.
- Include DOI links. No more than three heading levels.
- Footnotes rather than endnotes.
- More receptive to methodological detail and longer papers than REE.

### Journal of Urban Economics (JUE)
- Urban economics framing preferred. Housing, land use, transportation, local public finance.
- Abstract up to 100 words.
- Values structural approaches and general equilibrium reasoning more than pure real estate journals.
- Good for papers with spatial equilibrium or agglomeration themes.

### Journal of Housing Economics (JHE)
- Housing-focused: prices, affordability, policy, mortgage markets, household mobility.
- Moderate length abstracts.
- More receptive to descriptive and policy-oriented work than JUE or REE.

## Practical writing takeaways
- Top finance journals reward: sharp question, clear contribution, credible identification, economic magnitude, disciplined understatement.
- Opening pages should answer: what is the question, why does it matter, what is the design, what is the main finding, how does it change what we know.
- Do not overclaim novelty. Use "we contribute to" or "we provide evidence on" unless a stronger claim is verified.
- Every main table should be interpretable without forcing the reader back into the text.
- Real estate journals additionally reward: institutional depth, spatial awareness, policy relevance, and honest discussion of data limitations.
