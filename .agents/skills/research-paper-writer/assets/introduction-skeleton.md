# Introduction Skeleton

## Paragraph 1: Question and stakes
[Open with a specific, recent industry event, policy change, or market development that motivates the economic question (cite industry reports, news articles, or practitioner sources). Do not open with an abstract literature gap. Why does the answer matter for firms, investors, households, or policymakers? Ground it in real-world importance. Avoid: "Despite the importance of X, little is known about Y."]

## Paragraph 2: Mechanism or friction
[What economic force generates the testable prediction? Name the friction explicitly: information asymmetry, moral hazard, adverse selection, search friction, regulatory distortion, behavioral bias, market segmentation, etc. State the prediction: "If [mechanism], then we expect [outcome] because [logic]."]

## Paragraph 3: Setting, data, and design
[What empirical setting allows you to test this? Why is the setting informative for identification? Name the data sources. State the source of variation and the identification strategy in 2-3 sentences. The reader should understand the design before seeing results.]

## Paragraph 4: Main findings with magnitude
[State the main result quantitatively. "We find that X increases Y by Z%, which represents [economic benchmark (e.g., one-third of the sample standard deviation, equivalent to $X per firm)]." Include one or two secondary findings that reinforce the story. Do not just report sign and significance.]

## Paragraph 5: Robustness and mechanism (brief)
[2-3 sentences: "The result is robust to [key alternatives]. We provide evidence that the effect operates through [mechanism] rather than [alternative channel], using [specific test]." Do not list every robustness check; just the highlights.]

## Paragraph 6: Contribution and closest literature
[Name the 2-3 closest papers explicitly. For each, state the specific dimension of differentiation: mechanism, identification, data, setting, or implication. Use "we contribute to" or "we provide new evidence on," not "we are the first to" unless verified. If the paper bridges two literatures, explain what the bridge reveals.]


## Paragraph 7: Broader implication and roadmap
[What is the economic lesson beyond the specific finding? Is there a policy implication, an implication for theory, or an open question for future work? Then: "The remainder of the paper is organized as follows. Section II describes... Section III presents... Section IV discusses..." Keep the roadmap to 2-3 sentences.]
