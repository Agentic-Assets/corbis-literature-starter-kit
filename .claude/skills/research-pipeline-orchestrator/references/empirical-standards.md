# Empirical Standards Overview

This is a high-level checklist for assessing where a project stands relative to publication-grade empirical standards. Each specialized skill has a more detailed version tailored to its stage.

## Design stage
- Is the estimand clearly defined?
- Is there a credible source of variation?
- Is the comparison group economically sensible?
- Are the main threats identified and addressable?
- Is the design current with modern methods (staggered DiD, pretrend sensitivity, etc.)?

## Data stage
- Are the data sources accessible and documented?
- Are identifiers and merge keys reliable?
- Is the sample large enough for the proposed design?
- Are variable definitions precise and reproducible?
- Is date alignment correct (no look-ahead bias)?

## Analysis stage
- Does each table answer a clear question?
- Are results reported with economic magnitudes, not just statistical significance?
- Does each robustness check address a named threat?
- Is mechanism evidence distinguished from heterogeneity?
- Are standard errors correct for the design (clustering, spatial, etc.)?

## Writing stage
- Is the contribution front-loaded in the introduction?
- Are claims calibrated to what the design can support?
- Is the related literature organized by intellectual contribution, not topic label?
- Are tables and figures self-contained and interpretable?

## Revision stage
- Are referee concerns triaged by severity?
- Are critical identification issues addressed first?
- Have claims been narrowed where the design is weaker than originally stated?
- Does every claimed change actually appear in the revised manuscript?
